Clinical Science, Technology and Medicine Summer Internship at Stanford Anesthesia Summer Institute - Intern

July 2025 - July 2025

Throughout the program, I was introduced to core principles of clinical medicine through lectures, hands-on labs, patient case studies, and simulation exercises. We began with the fundamentals of patient interaction‚Äîdeveloping communication, empathy, and professionalism during clinical encounters.

A major focus was understanding the lived experiences of patients with complex conditions including chronic pain (rheumatoid arthritis), hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, lupus, bruxism, and respiratory diseases such as asthma, COPD, and emphysema. These sessions emphasized the need for mutual trust between patient and clinician‚Äîbuilt through listening, validating concerns, and engaging patients in their own care. I learned how these conditions affect patients beyond physical symptoms, influencing their daily routines, mental health, and long-term goals. We explored how ethical care requires not only informed clinical decisions but also respecting patient autonomy and values.

In parallel, we developed clinical understanding of these diseases through targeted labs and lectures. I learned to interpret EKGs (especially in cardiomyopathy), perform intravenous access and phlebotomy (essential for managing autoimmune and respiratory conditions), use glucose monitors (relevant for managing comorbid diabetes), and interpret x-rays (key in assessing emphysema and COPD). I also practiced intubation and suturing, reinforcing procedural skills vital in both emergency and routine care.

A team-based simulation challenged us to design a healthcare solution for patients affected by a natural disaster. We conducted interviews with simulated patients to identify real concerns, and created a response strategy tailored to their needs‚Äîstrengthening my collaborative and problem-solving abilities.

This experience deepened my understanding of both the clinical and human sides of medicine‚Äîhighlighting that effective care comes from both medical competency and shared understanding between patient and provider.
